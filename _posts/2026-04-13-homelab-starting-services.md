A homelab is easiest to enjoy when it solves one annoying problem first.

## Keep the host quiet
Use hardware that can stay on without being distracting. A small mini PC or old tower is fine if it is stable and easy to access.

## Add one thing you will use
DNS filtering, file sync, backups, or a simple dashboard are all better than a pile of unfinished experiments.

## Make recovery boring
Snapshots and backups matter more than specs. If something breaks, you want to fix it quickly and move on.

The best first lab is the one you can trust enough to forget about most days.
